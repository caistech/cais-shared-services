## Questions they ask during evaluation

### Compliance confidence
- "Will council actually accept this report?"
- "What's your false negative rate? If AI misses a fire rating issue, who's liable?"
- "How do you handle state variants to NCC?"

### Workflow fit
- "Do I upload PDF plans or do I need DWG/CAD files?"
- "Can I re-run when plans change without paying again?"
- "Does it export to my document management system?"

### Pricing
- "Does unused run volume roll over?"
- "Can my consultant white-label this for their clients?"
- "Is Enterprise worth it at my volume?"

## Proof points that close deals

- Sample PDF report (before pricing conversation)
- Specific NCC citations in output (not "the code requires...")
- Monash partnership logo
- Councils where reports have been accepted (collect testimonials)
- 98% accuracy claim — needs methodology backing for technical buyers

## Stall points

### Stall #1: "Can I trust AI for compliance?"
- Where: first-run evaluation
- What's happening: legitimate professional skepticism
- What unblocks it: show the report before asking for payment; highlight cross-validation (Tier 1 fire/structural/bushfire use secondary model)

### Stall #2: "My consultant won't like this"
- Where: mid-funnel
- What's happening: consultants are gatekeepers, not just competitors
- What unblocks it: white-label offer — consultants use it with their brand, MMC Build takes margin

### Stall #3: "Not enough volume for Professional tier"
- Where: price-tier selection
- What's happening: Basic 10/mo feels risky; Pro 30/mo feels wasteful
- What unblocks it: 2-month free trial is explicit; emphasize trial on all Basic → Pro upgrades

### Stall #4: "What about state variants?"
- Where: technical evaluation
- What's happening: NSW/VIC/QLD have amendments to NCC; buyers want confidence
- What unblocks it: publish state-variant coverage explicitly; don't leave it implied

## Conversion triggers

- Running first free trial report on a real current project
- Seeing a specific NCC section citation that matches what their consultant would have flagged
- Receiving council acceptance of the AI-generated report

## Expansion triggers

- Hitting Basic monthly limit in first 2 weeks → push to Pro
- Multiple users from same firm signing up independently → push to Enterprise / multi-seat
- Using MMC Comply then asking about Build/Quote → pipeline expansion natural path

## Churn signals

- No runs in first 14 days after signup
- Multiple failed uploads (document format/quality issues) → support intervention needed
- Volume dropping below 50% of tier allowance for 2+ months
- Consultant partner churning (takes their client pipeline with them)
