# Corporate AI Solutions — Market Positioning

_Last updated: 2026-04-21 (migrated from memory:project_messaging_reframe.md, 2026-03-23)_

## Core narrative

**The Unicorn Is the Factory.** The factory that builds AI companies at near-zero marginal cost.

Individual products are inventory. The factory is the valuable entity. The portfolio compounds to $200M+ ARR / $2B+ valuation.

## Territory we own

- **Near-zero marginal cost AI product manufacturing** — $5K build, 7-day cycle. No one else operates at this cost structure.
- **Factory-as-moat** — the studio is the durable asset, not any single product
- **Portfolio compounding** — 38 platforms shipped; economics validated at unit level
- **Long Tail AI Venture Studio** thesis — $1M ARR × many products > unicorn-or-nothing

## Territory that's contested

- "AI-native" — everyone claims this now. Don't lead with it.
- "Build fast" — standard startup claim. Lead with factory economics instead.
- "Venture studio" — crowded category. Differentiate with factory + portfolio math, not studio lineage.

## Territory we've ceded (intentionally)

- Unicorn-chasing, winner-take-all framing
- Single-product deep moats (we're explicitly a portfolio play)
- Enterprise sales cycles as the primary motion
- VC-track fundraising theater

## Narrative numbers (canonical)

Use these exact figures in investor/landing copy:

- **38 platforms** built (not 17, not "dozens")
- **$5K** build cost, **7-day** cycle
- **200 products × $1M avg ARR = $200M portfolio ARR**
- **10-15x SaaS portfolio multiple = $2B+ valuation**
- Individual exits at 6-10x revenue = "optional liquidity events", not the strategy

## Messaging DO / DON'T

✅ "The unicorn is the factory, not the product."
✅ "Portfolio economics at factory cost structure."
✅ "Individual exits are optional liquidity events."
✅ "Inventory compounds; factories scale."

❌ "We don't need unicorns." (retired 2026-03-23 — contradicts institutional scale)
❌ "Venture farming, not venture capital." (retired — lifestyle-business signal)
❌ "Small wins." (retired — caps ambition)
❌ "Lifestyle business optimising for small exits."

## White space

- **Factory metrics dashboards** — no competitor publicly publishes per-product unit economics across a portfolio. Opportunity for radical transparency.
- **Portfolio-as-asset narrative for acquirers** — strategic buyers understand product acquisition but not factory acquisition. Room to educate.
